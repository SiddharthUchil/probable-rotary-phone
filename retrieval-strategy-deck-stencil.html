Retrieval Strategy · 02One picture
The strategy in one picture

Understand → route → retrieve → check.

question + conversation→ detect language · EN / FR→ make it self-contained · new→ route by difficulty
Fast path · ~80% of questions

Keyword + meaning-based search combined (hybrid BM25 + vector, RRF) over enriched chunks, then a second-pass AI ranker re-orders the top 50–100 candidates.

Complex path · ~20%

An AI planner breaks the question into sub-searches and runs them in parallel (Azure agentic retrieval) — it reads the conversation natively. Behind a flag, falls back to the fast path.

merge→ access control · latest version · freshness→ groundedness check
The safety net

Every answer must cite a real retrieved article. Weak support → one retry with a rewritten query → otherwise the assistant says so and escalates to a human rather than guessing.

Retrieval Strategy · 03Why it wins
Why this wins · the head-to-head evidence

Retrieval is won in three places.

Across every rigorous comparison, this two-stage setup beats every exotic alternative. The gains come from three levers — not from swapping architectures.

01
What you index

Give every chunk its context (title, summary, category) before indexing. Cut retrieval failures by 67% in published tests — nearly free here, the enrichment already exists.

02
How you rank

A second-pass AI ranker is the single biggest lift: Recall@5 0.695 → 0.816. It's also multilingual — which carries the EN/FR split.

03
How you route

Spend the expensive AI only where it pays: +16 pts on complex questions, ~0 on simple ones. Routing is what makes that affordable.

The discipline A 300–500-question golden set gates every change measure first · ship second

Update · 2026·06·12New findings
New this update · three outside sources, stress-tested

The "LLM Wiki" idea —
checked against our plan.

A widely shared pattern (Karpathy's gist, an open-source app, a memory-focused extension) argues: skip search — have the AI keep all knowledge in a living wiki it reads directly. We verified the claims independently and tested the fit.

Replace our search? No.

The pattern's own rule says it only works under ~100k tokens of knowledge. Our corpus is ~50× past that ceiling — by its own decision tree, search (RAG) is mandatory here.

Validate our plan? Yes.

Its recommended search stack is exactly our backbone — hybrid keyword + meaning search with reranking — independently re-invented at personal scale. No existing decision changes.

Teach us anything? Yes.

Its memory ideas exposed our real gap: follow-up questions in conversation weren't handled on the fast path. The fix is the next slide.

Update · 2026·06·12Multi-turn
New this update · the one substantive addition

"What about for Quebec?"

On its own, this follow-up finds nothing — the words that matter were said two turns ago. The fix: before searching, rewrite every follow-up into a complete, self-contained question using the conversation so far.

"what about for Quebec?"+ conversation history→ "how do I submit a disability claim in Quebec?"→ search
+10–20%Retrieval gain from rewriting · published benchmarks
≤15Turns of full history · rolling summary beyond
0New infrastructure · no memory store needed
Why this and not a "memory system"

Heavy agent-memory machinery is built for assistants that remember you for months. Our sessions are short (5–30 turns) — a cheap rewrite plus a sensible history limit captures the benefit without the complexity. The complex path already reads conversation history natively.
