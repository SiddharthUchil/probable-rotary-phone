~/ retrieval / 02_headline_verdict
Verdict
The headline answer

Your stack is the validated architecture — not a baseline to replace.

Across every rigorous comparison, two-stage hybrid + neural reranking beats every exotic single-stage alternative. The biggest wins aren't architecture swaps — they're three levers.

01

What you index

Contextual (situated) chunk enrichment — the clearest, cheapest, most-replicated lift not yet in the near-term plan.

02

How you rank

Reranker quality + candidate depth — the single largest lever in the whole stack (Recall@5 0.695 → 0.816).

03

How you route

Query-class routing — spend expensive techniques only on complex queries (+16 pts), skip them on simple lookups (~0).

caution

HyDE measurably hurts retrieval on fact-dense corpora like insurance/benefits content — it should be demoted from the roadmap, not piloted.

Why it backfires

Hypothetical documents hallucinate plausible-but-wrong specifics — plan numbers, amounts, dates — dragging retrieval toward the wrong neighborhood.

Where it works (not you)

HyDE's published wins are on open-web, underspecified queries. Your corpus of plan numbers and procedure codes is exactly the failure case.

If ever tested

Gate to short / vague queries only, and measure against the golden set first.

Tier 3 · skip or defer

GraphRAG & RAPTOR

defer · with trigger

A systematic RAG-vs-GraphRAG evaluation: graph/hierarchical indexing offers no lift for predominantly single-hop, FAQ-style corpora.

Loses on single-hop

Natural Questions F1: RAG 64.78 > Community-GraphRAG 63.01 > RAPTOR 60.04. RAG also wins detail questions (55.28 vs 46.88).

Wins only multi-hop

And only by ~2–3 points (MultiHop-RAG: 70.27 vs 67.02) — a thin payoff for a heavy system.

40–57× indexing cost

135s → 5,560–7,702s to build on the same corpus, and quality depends on the graph-construction model.

LazyGraphRAG — the exception, narrowly

It swept 96/96 pairwise comparisons in Microsoft's BenchmarkQED — but its edge is on global / aggregative questions ("summarize everything about X"), which a support chatbot rarely sees. Trigger to revisit: production logs show multi-hop or corpus-wide aggregate queries failing at volume.

no

8k → 120k context didn't help; even 1M tokens lost to structured retrieval. Your corpus is far too large anyway. RAG isn't being displaced at 5k docs — that story is for <100-doc corpora.

ColBERT / SPLADE

skip

No Azure-native support → a second index + duplicated ACL trimming. No head-to-head evidence it beats hybrid + rerank on this doc type. The reranker already gives token-level scoring at the top of the funnel.

Decision
Overall best pick

Report B is the foundation. Report A is folded in.

The deciding factor is the kind of evidence. Head-to-head tests on this corpus type beat proposer-paper wins on open-web benchmarks.

Why B wins the base

Three of A's six picks don't survive head-to-head testing (HyDE, RAG-Fusion, ColBERT), and A misses the two highest-value items entirely — contextual chunks and agentic retrieval — plus the reranker's outsized role.

What A keeps

Three lasting contributions carried into the plan: fast / slow tiering (converges with routing), Self-RAG-as-orchestration (groundedness gate, no retraining), and LLM data-handling governance.

Net: keep the benchmark-grounded verdicts as the spine; graft on the survey's operational discipline. Neither report alone is the plan — the reconciliation is.

~/ retrieval / gaps
Add
What's missing from the roadmap

Four additions — highest leverage first.

01

Query-class routing

The meta-finding: no technique wins everywhere. A cheap classifier in front of the retriever — with per-class A/B — is the highest-leverage architectural add. Stratify the golden set by complexity class too.

02

llm_faqs as a channel

~4,600 article-level question sets already exist — free doc2query. Index each FAQ question as searchable + vector, mapped to its article. Query-to-question matching is strong for FAQ corpora. Measure it.

03

Cross-language policy

Decide explicitly whether an EN query may retrieve a FR-only article. If yes → multilingual embeddings + multilingual reranker (MIRACL-validated). BM25 won't cross languages.

04

Reranker depth + model ablation

Candidate depth (20 / 50 / 100) × model (Azure SR · Cohere 3.5 · LLM-rerank). High-impact, measurable knobs — treat as first-class golden-set experiments.

The consolidated architecture

Detect → route → retrieve → gate.

user query→ language detect · en / fr→ query-class router

Fast path · ~80%

Hybrid BM25 + vector (RRF) over contextual chunks + llm_faqs channel → semantic ranker (depth 50–100).

Slow path · ~20%

Azure agentic retrieval — LLM query planning → parallel subqueries → per-subquery hybrid + rank. Behind a preview flag; falls back to fast path.

merge→ ACL trim · latest-version · freshness / persona profiles→ groundedness gate

Groundedness gate: answer must cite a retrieved Title + content_path → weak support retries with query-rewrite fallback → else abstain & escalate.

In one breath

Retrieval quality is won in what you index, how you rank, and how you route.

Contextually enriched, de-duplicated, PII-safe chunks; hybrid recall plus a strong reranker at the right depth; a cheap path for the 80% of simple lookups and LLM-planned retrieval for the complex 20%. Everything else — HyDE, multi-query, GraphRAG, late interaction — measured flat-to-negative here or cost platform-breaking complexity, so it waits in a triggered backlog. The golden set goes in first, because every number here was only discoverable through that loop.
